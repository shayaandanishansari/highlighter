Executive summary

Highlighter is a personal media project founded by Shayaan Danish that grew out of a student Instagram page — @fasttimeskhi, now @highlighter.world — into a proposed identity platform: a person represented by what they think and what they build, rather than a resume or a feed. Fourteen months of docs hold the principles steady (no user monetisation, ever; people only, never brands; slow accumulation over growth-hacking) while the scope has narrowed hard, from a launchpad for a half-dozen "paradigm-shifting" products down to one disciplined three-pillar tool. What's actually running today is smaller than any vision doc: a working Instagram carousel generator for final-year-project showcases, an early-stage Next.js app that is mostly scaffolding, and this landing-page repo, which is still empty.

The thesis, as it stands today

The Trinity

The clearest statement of what Highlighter is for comes from the April 2026 overview doc: "Highlighter says you are the sum of what you think (stories) + what you build (projects). Together with your profile, these form a complete and coherent portrait of a person. Not a resume. Not a feed. A portrait." Three pillars, treated as one identity rather than three separate features:

thinking

Stories

How you think — long-form writing, essays, tutorials. Rich text via Tiptap, categories, tags, cover image, per-story color theming, nested comments.

doing

Projects

What you build — showcases with a short description, external links, cover image, and a photo gallery. The next thing scheduled to actually get built.

being

Profile

Who you are — bio, social links, geography, affiliations. Ties the other two together into one portrait, not a résumé.

The URL model exists to enforce this: every piece of content lives at /{username}/{slug}, never /stories/{slug} — the person is always the primary context, the content types are discovery lenses onto that person, not owners of it. Two more principles recur everywhere: alignment over engagement (not optimised for time-on-site or virality), and a flat refusal of user monetisation — "the moment it enters the room, every decision changes." Moderation follows from the same instinct: posts go live immediately and a report queue handles the rest, rather than pre-publish approval — a deliberate safe-harbor stance, not an oversight.

Where it came from

Origin, phase by phase

Highlighter didn't start as a platform idea — it started as a succession problem. FAST Times Karachi was a real, functioning student media project (two magazine issues, live procom interviews with industry reps, 2023–24, at FAST-NUCES). As the founding team graduated, "what happens to the Instagram page" turned into a much bigger question about what a coherent system for creators and collaborators could look like.

A tension worth naming

Two visions, written a day apart

The two "Current Vision" documents were written on consecutive days and don't fully agree with each other:

highlighter-overview.md

"A tool for people… not a social network, not a content platform, not a portfolio site." Positions Highlighter as an identity portrait — Stories + Projects + Profile. Sustained by industry and education-sector backing. Viable at 15–30 people, "built to still make sense in 15 years."

1-4-2026 (Current Vision)

Strategy & Execution Summary.md

"A lightweight, insight-capture tool that helps users save highlights, connect ideas, and think with clarity." Closer to a notes / PKM app. Sustained by donations, sponsorships, a capped "stewardship salary." Ships on a 3-week sprint.

1-4-2026 (Current Vision)

Both keep the "no user monetisation" line and the founder-as-builder framing — but one is a portrait platform for other people's work, the other is closer to a personal thinking tool. The website/project codebase's own CLAUDE.md has effectively resolved this in practice: it implements the Trinity / portrait version (Stories, Projects, People, person-first URLs), not the insight-capture one. Whoever writes the landing page copy will need to pick a lane too — the code has already picked one.

Reading the self-dialogue

Ethics as a design constraint, not an afterthought

One archived conversation stands apart: an extended reflection on the responsibility of building something that shapes how people see their own identity.

"Underneath everything, the conversation is about responsibility: how to create something meaningful without becoming destructive… Highlighter becomes a case study for how ideas, identity, education, and power interact."

Concretely, this produced two commitments visible later in the docs: the phase plan is treated as "a governance and safety mechanism, not just a roadmap," explicitly meant to stop the project running to "phase infinity"; and a requirement for "structural humility" — feedback loops that keep a self-expression tool from calcifying into an ideology. It's also the source of the pause-and-return decision: finish nearer-term obligations (an assignment, a GitHub repo, FYP reports) before resuming Highlighter, on the reasoning that clearer thinking there improves leadership everywhere else.

What actually exists — part one

The Forms Project: the thing that's really shipping

The most concrete artifact in the whole project isn't a doc — it's a small working app: a client-side studio (Vite, MIT-licensed) that turns FYP Submission Form responses into editable 1080×1080 Instagram carousel decks, exported via html2canvas. No server, no accounts, nothing leaves the browser. It's the literal implementation of "Keep FAST Times as forefront" from the original phase plan.

What actually exists — part two

The main app: website/project

Tech stack, per its own package.json and CLAUDE.md: Next.js 15.5 (App Router), React 18.3, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS 3, shadcn/ui on Radix primitives, and Tiptap 2.6 for the rich-text story editor. There's a deliberate house rule against single-maintainer npm packages — cn() / cva() are hand-rolled in src/lib/utils.ts, icons are inlined SVG in src/lib/icons.tsx rather than pulled from lucide-react. It began life as a Firebase Studio starter (Firebase/Genkit/Gemini have since been fully ripped out — a stray README line is the only trace left).

The gap that matters most

Almost every backend file is a literal // TODO stub with an empty body. drizzle/schema.ts — meant to be the "single source of truth" per its own CLAUDE.md — defines zero tables. src/lib/db.ts, auth.ts, middleware.ts, every *.service.ts (story, project, profile, comment, search, upload), and every src/app/api/**/route.ts handler are all empty stubs. StoryEditor.tsx — the centerpiece Tiptap editor — currently renders <div>Editor coming soon.</div>. The data model exists only as plain TypeScript types (User, Story, Project, Comment, Submission in src/models/index.ts), not yet backed by a real schema.

What is real: the full page/route skeleton (route groups (auth) and (main), the dynamic [username]/[slug] content route, admin / donate / transparency pages), the complete shadcn/ui component library, and a fully-built Navbar.tsx (black sticky header, settings / battery / paperclip icons left, Home / Stories / Projects / People nav plus an orange Account button right, active-state styling). The homepage itself is still a bare hero placeholder. Recent commit history — a cleanup pass, a navbar/footer refactor, a stub restructuring around the username-first URL model, then a run of unlabeled "auto: claude session checkpoint" commits — reads as iterative, Claude-assisted scaffolding, with Projects next in line to actually get built (DB → API → UI), then Stories, then Auth.

What actually exists — part three

This repo, and the playground

website/v1_landing — this repository — is a fresh scaffold: one commit ("exploring how to present a clean landing page"), a .gitignore, and a completely empty README.md. No framework, no package.json, no copy or design drafted yet. Its remote points at github.com/shayaandanishansari/highlighter.

playground/ is a scratch folder, not a structured app. Its most useful signal: playground/resend/send_email.js, a minimal script confirming transactional email works via the Resend API, sending from noreply@highlighter.world — meaning the highlighter.world domain is live and email-capable. The folder also holds the current brand assets — logo.svg, logo-dark.svg, logo-icon.svg — a stylized marker mark in orange (#FFA500) and black/dark-grey, in light and dark variants plus an icon-only mark.

Security note — worth acting on, not just noting

playground/text.txt currently holds plaintext, live-looking credentials in the clear: a Gmail app password and a full Google OAuth2 client JSON blob (client ID/secret, project ID highlighter-492318, redirect URIs for both localhost:3000 and highlighter.world). playground/.env similarly holds a live RESEND_API_KEY, Google client ID/secret, and a JWT signing secret. playground/.gitignore excludes both from that folder's own git tracking, which helps only if playground is its own repo and nothing else backs the folder up unencrypted.

Worth confirming directly: that neither file has ever been committed anywhere, and that they're excluded from any sync/backup that isn't fully private. Rotating the Gmail app password and Google OAuth secret is cheap insurance if there's any doubt.

Beyond the Trinity

The wider constellation

Highlighter is consistently described as one tool among several related ones the founder is deliberately keeping separate — to merge only "if they naturally converge through long-term use":

EoN / Eon
A private counterpart to Highlighter — messy thinking and rehearsal before public articulation, without reputational risk. Also framed elsewhere as a decision/career-clarity tool.
Mirror / Shattered Glass
A private AI tool reflecting your own language patterns back to you over time — themes, drift, blind spots. Explicitly not diagnostic.
Debate mind-shift tool
Pre/post belief polling for debates — rewards updating your mind over "winning" the argument.
Truth Perspective
Compares lenses and assumptions rather than conclusions — makes disagreement legible instead of adversarial.
Belief investing
A non-monetary "conviction capital" system to back creators — a costlier, written signal replacing likes.
AI Buddy layer
Opt-in reflective companion UI — user-owned self-surveillance instead of platform surveillance. One-click off switch.
DSL search
A QuoteScript-inspired query language over Stories / Projects / People — recurs in nearly every doc as the planned power-user layer.
The Arc
An unrelated, same-voice side project: an 8-segment podcast format where a business guest's own answers reveal whether their stated mission matches how they operate.
Reading between the lines

Open threads

Scope has been shrinking, on purpose

An early launchpad list — Journeys.com, Sirway, HistoryOfTheEntireWorld, a drone-media football league — and a ~25-idea backlog have quietly dropped out of every doc after April 2026. Only the Trinity and its immediate satellites survive into the live codebase. Reads as the phase-discipline from the ethics conversation actually working.

Framing hasn't fully converged

"Portrait of a person" and "insight-capture tool" imply different UIs and different homepages. The codebase has picked the portrait version; the Cloudflare/sprint/donation plan from the Strategy doc hasn't been reconciled with it. Landing-page copy will force this choice into the open.

Data model is the biggest engineering gap

Both the dedicated schema spreadsheet and drizzle/schema.ts are empty placeholders. Nothing has actually modelled Stories / Projects / Profiles as tables yet, despite the Trinity being the entire thesis.

Sustainability model is still aspirational

"No user monetisation, ever" is firm and repeated everywhere. What replaces it — institutional backing, donations, a capped stewardship salary — has three different versions across the docs and no evidence yet of a real sponsor or institution.
